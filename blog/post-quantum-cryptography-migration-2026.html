Post-Quantum Cryptography Migration 2026: What Enterprises Need to Do

March 2026

Post-quantum cryptography (PQC) migration is no longer theoretical. With NIST finalizing standards and 2026 deadlines in view, enterprises holding long-term sensitive or regulated data need a clear plan for hybrid HSM and key management that can resist future quantum decryption.

Why post-quantum migration matters in 2026

Today’s public-key cryptography—RSA, elliptic curve—can be broken by sufficiently powerful quantum computers. NIST’s PQC project has selected algorithms (e.g. CRYSTALS-Kyber, CRYSTALS-Dilithium) that are designed to remain secure even against quantum attacks. For enterprises, 2026 is the year to align architecture with these standards: keys and data protected today may need to stay confidential for decades.

What enterprises need to do

  • Inventory long-lived data and keys — Identify what must be protected with PQC and over what timeframe.
  • Plan hybrid HSM and key management — Run classical and PQC algorithms side by side during transition; ensure key storage and vaulting support both.
  • Document and test recovery — Recovery validation (including Time to Clean Restore) should include PQC-backed vaults and keys.
