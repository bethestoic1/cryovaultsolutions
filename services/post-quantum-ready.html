Post-Quantum Ready Architecture (2026)

As NIST finalizes post-quantum cryptography (PQC) standards for 2026, organizations holding long-term sensitive or regulated data must plan for hybrid HSM and key-management architectures that resist future quantum decryption.

What post-quantum readiness means for enterprises

Today’s public-key crypto (RSA, ECC) will become vulnerable once large-scale quantum computers exist. PQC standards define replacement algorithms and migration paths. For enterprises, post-quantum readiness means:

  • Identifying long-lived data and keys that need PQC protection.
  • Designing hybrid HSM and key-management setups that can run classical and PQC algorithms during transition.
  • Planning key rotation and vaulting so you can prove compliance and integrity as standards evolve.
